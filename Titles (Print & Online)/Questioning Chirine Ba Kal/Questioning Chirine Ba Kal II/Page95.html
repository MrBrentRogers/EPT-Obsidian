    Originally Posted by Gronan of Simmerya
    Glad to hear it. Sometimes it's just nice to have a quiet day at home working on a project.

    I'm in the process of building one of the Central Valley track switch kits to see how it goes. Today I put in the stock rails and guardrails.
    Agreed. I'm finding that the more I disengage with the local gaming scene the happier and more productive I am. Going forward, there's going to be a lot more of the Free RPG Day sort of thing then what we did in years past with games being run for people who never bothered to say 'thank you' or help out. I have some really cool projects in the works, which are being done with the help and encouragement of my family, and if anyone wants to come along for the fun then they are quite welcome to join us - I think I can still amaze and astonish with my skills. I like building things, running games, and telling stories, and I'm going to keep on doing that; there are, I gather, some other folks who share that philosophy.

    In contrast, the latest Grand Scheme that's crossed my desk is a proposal that I donate my entire collection to the Strong Museum of Play. I'm still trying to figure out what prompted this notion, and what I'm supposed to get out of it. Oh, yeah, and why am I not running Tekumel game events at the FLGS every month for people? I'm told that playing in my games is a big prestige thing, and you can be a Big Name Gamer if you are one of my players.; so, I owe it to Tekumel fans to run games for them as they are entitled to my time and energy.

    Ah, right. Sure. Me, I thought we had more fun at our game on Barsoom, myself, and the kids really had fun in Lord Meren's Egypt. And the Skyline display looked really good, too; you should see what I have for the next event that I'm invited to...

    I wonder if Cleo's dry, yet?
