    Questions on "vats" and "vats-creatures":

    According to the Sourcebook, the priesthoods of "Ksarul, Gruganu, and the Horned One of Secrets of Livyanu" know how to create genetic aberrations which they and other clergies employ as slaves, servants, or soldiers, like the Qol or the Vorodla.

    01 - In your adventures, did you, Chirine, ever see these vats or visit the "labs" where these creatures are born/created?
    02 - Should we assume that most if not all of these vats and laboratories are located in the most secretive portions of the above-mentioned clergies' temples?
    03 - I assume that these vats/tanks are other-planar energy powered. Would that be correct? The Sourcebook suggests so.
    04 - Are creatures like the ones I mention above still being created, or were they all created way-back-when, like during the Latter Times, and have now become "standard species", in that they can reproduce naturally?
    05 - Through the centuries, the Qols, or whatever other vat-creature, that managed to escape, or get lost, or achieve some sort of independence of thought probably managed to create communities of their own, living hidden on the fringe of society and/or in the most remote areas of the world. Did you, Chirine, ever visit/stumble upon such communities of "under-people"? If yes, how did such encounter(s) go?

    Originally Posted by Horu hiFa'asu
    Honored Uncles, I have yet more questions...

    N'luss, Pe Choi, and Shen (oh my).. and other 'non humans' - where they ever popular with the players? It seems that Tekumel from a gaming perspective was very human-centric. Given the importance of social relationships this makes a bit of sense, the 'alien races' where actually 'alien' after all.

    N'luss, being 'bred' from humans originally, would seem to have no issues socially with other humans (other than the obvious drastic difference in size!). How are they regarded in polite society? Assuming they are accustomed to civilized lands. Are they regarded as 'pretty much the same as us' ? Would someone from Tsolyanu regard a N'luss similar to the way they regard someone from Livyanu?

    Reading a bit about the Pe Choi - it almost seems to hint at some kind of greater 'connection' between all Pe Choi that they are now cut off from - perhaps hinting at some kind of low level psychic ability across the species. Was there anything ever hinted at this in the game or is it my imagination running amok again?
    Not really. Three people played a Shen, a Pe Choi, and a Tinaliya over the years; that's it, as far as I know.

    As useful barbarian mercenaries / soldiers. Not very high status, but useful. No; a Livyani would be higher status.

    No, it was clearly stated, right up front, which is why we made very sure not to engage in unprovoked feuds with the 'wild' Pe Choi in the Chakas. Kill one Pe Choi, and all of the rest of them just might come after you.
